Baby Health Guide

Essential information and tips for keeping your baby healthy and safe

0-3 Months Health

  • First pediatric visit in the first week of life, then at 1 month
  • Hepatitis B vaccine within 24 hours of birth
  • 2-month visit: First major immunization series (DTaP, IPV, PCV, Hib, Rotavirus)
  • Watch for jaundice in first days
  • Emergency: Call pediatrician immediately for fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  • Monitor feeding and digestive issues
  • Regular check-ups to track growth and development

3-6 Months Health

  • Well-check visits at 4 and 6 months
  • 4-month shots: Second doses of 2-month vaccines
  • 6-month shots: Third doses of vaccines
  • First flu vaccine at 6 months (two doses, one month apart)
  • Teething begins: Watch for drooling and chewing
  • Discuss iron supplementation if exclusively breastfed
  • Track developmental milestones (rolling, pushing up, smiling, babbling)

6-12 Months Health

  • 6-month visit: Third doses of vaccines
  • Annual flu vaccine recommended
  • First teeth appear (6-10 months)
  • First dental visit by 12 months
  • Monitor developmental milestones
  • Watch for ear infections, especially after colds
  • Separation anxiety may begin around 8-9 months
  • Master sitting and begin crawling

12-24 Months Health

  • 12-month vaccines: MMR, Varicella, Hepatitis A
  • 15-18 months: DTaP booster, Hib and PCV if needed
  • 18 months: Second Hepatitis A dose
  • Autism screening at 18 and 24 months
  • Regular dental check-ups every 6 months
  • Monitor speech development (50+ words by 2 years)
  • Watch for temper tantrums and boundary testing
  • Track physical milestones (walking, running, kicking)
  • Expect 5-7 colds per year

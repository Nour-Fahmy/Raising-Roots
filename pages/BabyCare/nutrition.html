Breastfeeding Basics

Getting Started with Breastfeeding

Breastfeeding is a natural way to feed your baby, but it can take time to learn. Here are some essential tips:

  • Find a comfortable position for both you and your baby
  • Ensure proper latching technique
  • Feed on demand in the early weeks
  • Stay hydrated and maintain a healthy diet

Common Challenges

Many mothers face challenges while breastfeeding. Here's how to handle them:

  • Sore nipples - Use lanolin cream and ensure proper positioning
  • Low milk supply - Nurse frequently and stay hydrated
  • Engorgement - Use warm compresses and gentle massage

Formula Feeding Guide

Choosing the Right Formula

There are several types of formula available:

  • Standard cow's milk-based formula
  • Soy-based formula
  • Hypoallergenic formula
  • Specialized formulas for specific needs

Preparing and Storing Formula

Follow these guidelines for safe formula preparation:

  • Always wash your hands before preparation
  • Use clean, sterilized bottles
  • Follow the package instructions exactly
  • Store prepared formula properly

Introducing Solid Foods

When to Start Solids

Most babies are ready for solid foods around 6 months. Look for these signs:

  • Can sit up with support
  • Shows interest in food
  • Can hold head steady
  • Has lost the tongue-thrust reflex

First Foods to Try

Start with these simple, single-ingredient foods:

  • Iron-fortified single-grain cereal
  • Pureed vegetables (sweet potato, carrots)
  • Pureed fruits (banana, avocado)
  • Pureed meats

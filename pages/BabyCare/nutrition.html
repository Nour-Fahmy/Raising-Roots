Baby Nutrition Guide

Essential information and tips for healthy nutrition and feeding

0-3 Months Nutrition

  • Feed 8-12 times per 24 hours (every 2-3 hours)
  • Exclusive breastfeeding recommended for first 6 months
  • Breastfed infants need 400 IU vitamin D daily
  • Monitor wet diapers and weight gain
  • Formula-fed babies: Follow package instructions exactly
  • Feed on demand, watch for hunger cues
  • Ensure proper latch for breastfeeding
  • No water or other liquids needed

3-6 Months Nutrition

  • Continue exclusive breastfeeding or formula
  • Feedings may space to every 3-4 hours
  • Not ready for solids before 6 months
  • Continue vitamin D for breastfed babies
  • Do not add cereal to bottles unless directed by doctor
  • Watch for growth spurts and increased hunger
  • Digestive system still developing
  • No juice or other beverages needed

6-12 Months Nutrition

  • Introduce solid foods around 6 months
  • Start with single-ingredient purees or soft foods
  • Introduce one new food every 3 days
  • Include potential allergens in small amounts
  • Breast milk/formula still primary nutrition source
  • Offer solids after milk feeding
  • Provide sips of water with meals
  • Avoid juice and sugary drinks
  • Watch for signs of food allergies

12-24 Months Nutrition

  • Transition to whole cow's milk at 12 months
  • Three meals and 1-2 healthy snacks daily
  • Encourage self-feeding with supervision
  • Offer variety of foods from all food groups
  • Limit juice to 4 oz per day, avoid sugary drinks
  • Portions should be toddler-sized
  • Continue to avoid choking hazards
  • Introduce open cup for practice
  • Watch for iron-rich foods
  • Family mealtimes important for modeling

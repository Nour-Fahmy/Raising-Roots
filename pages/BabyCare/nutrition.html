Baby Nutrition Guide

Essential information and tips for your baby's nutrition and feeding

0-3 Months Nutrition

  • Exclusive breastfeeding or formula feeding
  • Feed every 2-3 hours (8-12 times per day)
  • Watch for hunger cues (rooting, sucking hands)
  • Feeding sessions typically last 20-40 minutes
  • Burp baby after each feeding
  • Spit-up is normal
  • Expect 5-7 ounces weight gain per week

3-6 Months Nutrition

  • Introduce solid foods around 6 months
  • Continue breastfeeding or formula
  • 4-6 feedings per day
  • Start with single-ingredient purees
  • Introduce common allergens one at a time
  • Can introduce small amounts of water
  • Develop self-feeding skills

6-12 Months Nutrition

  • 3 meals and 2-3 snacks per day
  • Can continue breastfeeding if desired
  • Transition to whole milk (if not breastfeeding)
  • Offer variety of foods from all food groups
  • Small portions (1-2 tablespoons per food)
  • Encourage self-feeding with utensils
  • Offer water between meals
  • Avoid honey, choking hazards, and added sugar

12-24 Months Nutrition

  • 3 meals and 2-3 snacks per day
  • Can continue breastfeeding if desired
  • Transition to whole milk (if not breastfeeding)
  • Offer variety of foods from all food groups
  • Small portions (1-2 tablespoons per food)
  • Encourage self-feeding with utensils
  • Offer water between meals
  • Avoid honey, choking hazards, and added sugar

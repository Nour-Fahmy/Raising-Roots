Baby Sleep Guide

Essential information and tips for establishing healthy sleep habits

0-3 Months Sleep

  • 14-17 hours of sleep per day in short stretches
  • Always place baby on back for sleep
  • Use firm, flat sleep surface with fitted sheet only
  • Share room with parents (not bed) for first 6 months
  • Establish gentle bedtime routine to help with day/night confusion
  • No loose bedding, pillows, or toys in sleep area
  • Room temperature should be 68-72Â°F
  • Newborn day/night confusion is normal

3-6 Months Sleep

  • 12-16 hours of total sleep daily
  • May sleep longer stretches at night (5-6 hours)
  • Stop swaddling when showing signs of rolling
  • Use wearable blanket (sleep sack) instead of swaddling
  • Establish consistent nap and bedtime routine
  • Night wakings for feeding still common
  • Begin to develop self-soothing skills
  • Maintain safe sleep practices

6-12 Months Sleep

  • 12-15 hours of sleep in 24-hour period
  • 2-3 naps during the day
  • Lower crib mattress as baby starts pulling up
  • No soft toys, bumpers, or loose blankets in crib
  • May experience sleep regressions due to milestones
  • Bedtime routine important (bath, book, lullaby)
  • May start standing in crib
  • Can roll over in sleep safely

12-24 Months Sleep

  • 11-14 hours of sleep per day
  • Transition to one nap (1-3 hours) around 15-18 months
  • Consistent bedtime routine important
  • May develop sleep associations or fears
  • Consider toddler bed if climbing out of crib
  • May experience nightmares or night terrors
  • Bedtime resistance common
  • Can introduce small security object after 12 months
